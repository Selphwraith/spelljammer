📖 Captain's Handbook — How to Play
⚓ Your Mission
You command a spelljamming vessel for the Imperial Elven Navy, launching from Refuge — a hidden IEN base sealed inside an artificial Crystal Sphere. The known spheres are under threat: an Elder Brain Flagship drifts through Elder Space, consuming all conscious thought in its path.

Your orders: travel the Phlogiston, recover all six Astral Chart Fragments hidden across six Crystal Spheres, assemble the completed chart, unlock Elder Space, and destroy the Flagship before it reaches the inner spheres. Each Fragment is guarded — you'll need to find clues at each sphere's port before you can locate and fight for them.
🗺 Navigation & The Map
The main screen shows the Phlogiston — the flammable sea between Crystal Spheres. Spheres appear as glowing circles.

To travel: tap any sphere to chart a course. Your ship animates across the Phlogiston.
To dock: tap your current sphere (the one you're already at) to enter port.
Locked spheres show a lock icon — some require story progress or Fragments to reach.

Waypoints — small icons scattered across the Phlogiston — are supply caches, relay stations, and derelict vessels drifting on void currents. Tap one to intercept it for supplies, air, or crew. They're reachable from any heading since your ship can adjust course mid-void.

Air Supply counts down during travel. If it hits zero your crew suffocates — you lose. Refill air at any port before long journeys. The Air Recycler upgrade doubles how long your air lasts.
🧩 Astral Chart Fragments
Fragments are not simply collected by visiting spheres — they must be hunted down.

Step 1 — Find the Clue. Each sphere holds an NPC who knows something. Dock at a port and speak to them. They'll describe a hidden location within or near that sphere — a vault, a derelict, a dark mote, a broker's cache — where a Fragment is being guarded.

Step 2 — Mark the Location. Accept the clue to mark the Fragment Site on your chart. The site then appears as a glowing marker you can navigate to directly.

Step 3 — Defeat the Guardian. Each Fragment is protected by a named Guardian — a powerful enemy unique to that sphere. Corsair captains, iron wardens, void prophets, ancient vault-keepers. These are harder than standard encounters. Come prepared.

Step 4 — Claim the Fragment. Defeat the Guardian and the Fragment is yours. Collect all 6 to unlock Elder Space and face the final battle.

Tip: Guardians are significantly tougher than regular enemies. Stock up on hull repair items, upgrade your weapons, and consider having crew-restore items on hand before engaging one.
📊 Your Four Resources
Losing any one of these ends your voyage:

⚔ Hull — Your ship's structural integrity. Reaches 0? The ship breaks apart — defeat. Repair at port or use hull items mid-combat.
👥 Crew — Your sailors. If crew reaches 0, the helm goes dark and the ship drifts — defeat, even at full hull. Crew can be lost to enemy boarding attacks and certain specials. Hire crew at port.
💨 Air Supply — Consumed during travel. Runs out mid-voyage? Everyone suffocates. Always refill before leaving port.
⚜ Gold — Earned from combat loot and selling cargo. Spent on repairs, upgrades, weapons, and supplies. Never drops below 0 — but you can run out.
⚔ Combat — The Basics
Random encounters fire during travel. Combat is strictly turn-based — you act first, then the enemy acts, then back to you.

The combat screen splits into two panels at the bottom:
Left panel — Your Weapons: tap any ready weapon card to fire it. Green glow = ready. Dimmed with a number = reloading that many rounds.
Right panel — Enemy Systems: shows the enemy's named weapons with threat ratings (◆◆◆◆◆). High-threat weapons are worth disabling.

Hull bars track both ships above the combat log. The enemy also shows a crew bar — drain their crew to 0 and their specials go offline and attacks weaken.
⚔ Combat — Your Actions
All actions appear below the weapon panels:

🔥 Fire Weapon — Tap a weapon card. Each weapon has a reload time (shown in rounds). The Ballista fires every round; heavier weapons take 1–4 rounds to reload. You can only fire one weapon per turn unless you have special upgrades.

🌀 Evasive Maneuver — 50% chance to dodge the next enemy attack entirely. Use before a known heavy hit. Seeking weapons (Githyanki Silver Swords) cannot be dodged.

🔧 Emergency Repair — Spends 2 Crew to restore up to 25 Hull. Be careful — losing crew is dangerous. Only appears when hull is damaged and crew > 2.

Item Buttons — Any usable item in your cargo hold appears here. Tap to use it instantly.

💨 Flee — 50% chance of escape. On failure, the enemy still attacks. Some items (Emergency Beacon) guarantee a successful flee.
⚔ Combat — Special Upgrades
Purchase these from port shops to unlock powerful combat options:

🎯 Linked Fire (Fire Control System upgrade) — Fire two ready weapons simultaneously once per few rounds.
🌊 Volley Fire (Volley Array upgrade) — Fire all ready weapons at once. Replaces Linked Fire when installed.
⚡ Chain Reaction Crystal — Every hit has a 30% chance to trigger a free bonus shot.
🔄 Rapid Reload — All weapon cooldowns reduced by 1 round permanently.
🚀 Ship Signature Abilities
Each ship class (except the starting Pinnace) has a built-in signature ability that upgrades up to three tiers. Buy upgrades at any port.

🦑 Squid Ship — Tentacle Grapple: Binds the enemy, halving their attack damage for 2 full turns. No damage at Tier 1 — pure defense. Tier 2 adds 8 direct damage. Tier 3 stuns the enemy entirely for one turn plus 15 damage. Cooldown: 5–6 rounds.

🔨 Hammership — Siege Ram: Rams the enemy for 2d10–4d10 impact damage — but you take 2–5 hull damage yourself from the collision. High risk, high reward. Tier 3 permanently reduces the enemy's max hull by 10. Cooldown: 5–6 rounds.

⚓ Man-o-War — Starfire Broadside: Fires all ready weapons simultaneously in one devastating salvo — no individual cooldown penalties applied. Tier 2 adds a +20% damage bonus to each shot. Tier 3 also suppresses the enemy's next attack. The capital ship's defining move. Cooldown: 5–7 rounds.
👁 Enemy Weapon Systems
Every enemy has named weapon systems shown in the right combat panel. Threat ratings run from ◆ (low) to ◆◆◆◆◆ (extreme).

Dangerous weapons to watch for:
Beholder — Disintegration Ray ◆◆◆◆◆ — Each hit permanently reduces your max hull. Disable it immediately.
Nautiloid — Psionic Lancer ◆◆◆◆ — Stuns your crew, skipping your next turn.
Astral Leviathan — Void Breath Cannon ◆◆◆◆◆ — Adds +2 reload rounds to all your weapons.
Neogi — Web Cannon ◆◆◆ — Disables a random player weapon for 2 rounds.
Scro — Rage Cannon ◆◆◆◆◆ — Damage escalates every round it keeps firing. Kill it or disable it fast.
Far Realm Stalker — Reality Shear ◆◆◆◆◆ — Ignores all your damage resistance entirely.
Elder Brain — Void Singularity ◆◆◆◆◆ — Pierces resistance AND permanently shreds max hull by 8 per hit.
⬡ Disabling Enemy Weapons
Use a Phase Disruptor or Phase Disruptor Mk.II from your cargo to enter targeting mode. All enemy weapon cards glow gold — tap the one you want to shut down. It goes dark for 2–3 turns and cannot fire. The Mk.II disables for 3 turns instead of 2.

Tip: always disable the highest-threat weapon first — especially Disintegration Rays and Rage Cannons. A disabled weapon is far more valuable than one point of extra damage.
💊 Enemy Healing & Buffs
Many enemies have a heal or buff ability that triggers when their hull drops low. This fires as a separate event — they spend their action recovering instead of attacking you.

Watch for the warning in the enemy panel: "⚠ [Ability] approaching…" — that's your cue to burst them down before they recover.

Notable examples: Astral Leviathan heals 25 HP once per fight. Neogi sacrifices 2 of its own crew to heal 20 HP. Elder Brain heals 40 HP below half health. Githyanki gains +3 damage resistance for 2 turns instead of healing.

Killing enemy crew with the Psionic Lance weapon also suppresses their healing specials — zero crew disables all special abilities.
⚓ Port — Supplies & Arms
Tap your current sphere to dock. The port has two tabs:

Supplies & Arms: repair hull, hire crew, refill air, buy weapons, purchase ship upgrades, and buy usable items. Prices vary by location and faction reputation.
Sell Cargo: convert looted trade goods, components, and artifacts into gold. Always sell before buying — you may have more than you think.

Faction Reputation affects prices: high IEN rep gives you discounts; negative Scro rep may add a surcharge. Build rep by completing local quests and making allied choices.
🚀 Ship Classes
Upgrade your ship at port shipyards. Larger ships have more weapon slots, higher hull, and bigger crews — but cost significantly more gold:

Pinnace — Starting ship. 2 weapon slots, 65 hull, 6 crew. Fast and cheap but fragile. No signature ability.
🦑 Squid Ship (350g) — 3 weapon slots, 80 hull, 8 crew. Gains Tentacle Grapple — a powerful defensive ability that halves enemy damage for 2 turns.
🔨 Hammership (700g) — 4 weapon slots, 100 hull, 12 crew. Gains Siege Ram — brutal high-damage ram attack with self-damage risk.
⚓ Man-o-War (1,400g) — 5 weapon slots, 130 hull, 16 crew. Gains Starfire Broadside — fires all ready weapons simultaneously. The ultimate endgame vessel.

Tip: upgrade when you have reliable income and at least one good weapon already purchased.
🔫 Weapons Guide
Weapons range from basic to devastating. Each occupies one weapon slot:

Ballista — Free. Fires every round. Low damage (1d6) but never reloads — reliable bread-and-butter.
Light Cannon — 1 round reload. Decent damage (2d6). Good second weapon for Pinnace.
Heavy Cannon — 2 round reload. Strong damage (3d8). Standard mid-game weapon.
Psionic Lance — Unique. Attacks enemy crew directly, not hull. Great for suppressing enemy specials on Nautiloids and Beholders.
Inferno Cannon — Applies burn DoT. Enemy takes fire damage every turn for 3 rounds.
Null Bolt — Suppresses enemy specials for 2 turns in addition to hull damage.
Far Realm weapons (Void Torrent, Warp Cannon, etc.) — Extreme damage but destabilize your hull slightly. Endgame power.
📦 Cargo & Usable Items
Items found in loot or purchased at port occupy your cargo hold. Three categories:

Hull Repair (use in combat or at sea):
🔩 Emergency Repair Kit — +20 hull
🧪 Leviathan Blood Vial — +30 hull
⚗️ Astral Forge Elixir — +50 hull, also clears burn damage

Crew Restore:
🍺 Crew's Courage Mead — +3 crew
💉 Spelljammer Stimpack — +6 crew
🎖️ IEN Surge Rations — +10 crew

Combat Utility:
🔮 Psionic Amplifier — Stuns enemy, skips their next action
💥 Null Sphere Grenade — Stuns enemy AND deals 15 direct damage
⬡ Phase Disruptor — Enter targeting mode, disable one enemy weapon for 2 turns
⬡ Phase Disruptor Mk.II — Same but 3 turns disabled
🔔 Emergency Beacon — 100% guaranteed flee, no roll needed
🩹 Alchemist's Mend — Restores 20 hull AND 1 crew simultaneously
🫧 Void Salve — Clears all burn damage, restores 15 hull

Equippables (install from Cargo Manifest tab for permanent buffs):
Hull plates, resist plating, crew quarters, and more. Once installed, they're permanent.
📋 Local Quests & Reputation
Many ports offer local quests — side missions that reward gold, items, or reputation. Check the quest board when docked. Quests typically ask you to defeat a specific enemy, deliver cargo, or visit a nearby sphere.

Faction Reputation builds separately with the IEN, Scro, Neogi, and others. Higher rep with a faction unlocks better prices at their ports, exclusive items, and special dialogue. Negative rep means surcharges and potentially hostile encounters.
💀 Permanent Hull Damage
Certain enemy weapons don't just deal damage — they permanently reduce your maximum hull. This is not restored by repairs or healing items.

Beholder — Disintegration Ray: −4 max hull per hit.
Elder Brain — Void Singularity Pulse: −8 max hull per hit.
World Bosses — Sphere Unmaking Pulse: −8 max hull per hit.

Two ways to restore lost max hull:
🔩⚡ Void Welder (field kit) — A consumable item that restores 20 max hull per use. Found as rare loot from Beholders and the Elder Brain, or purchased at ports with a Structural Drydock. Carry one into any Beholder fight.
⚙️ Structural Drydock (port service) — Available at Refuge, Realmspace, and Krynnspace. Fully restores all lost max hull for 8 gold per lost point. The only way to fully recover after a brutal fight.

Tip: if you're below your ship's max hull cap, the Drydock option will appear in the port shop automatically.
💾 Saving & Loading
The game auto-saves every time you arrive at a new sphere or leave port. You can also press the 💾 Save button in the top header at any time to manually save to one of three slots.

Load a saved voyage from the main menu's Load Game option. Auto-save is always overwritten — manual saves let you keep multiple voyages.
💡 Captain's Tips
• Never leave port with less than 15 air. Long voyages kill captains who forget to refill.
• Sell cargo before buying supplies. You may already have enough gold.
• Crew matters as much as hull. 0 crew = defeat even at full hull. Protect them.
• Disable the scariest weapon first. Beholder Disintegration Ray and Scro Rage Cannon become exponentially worse the longer they fire.
• Evasive Maneuver before a known big hit. It's wasted after the attack lands.
• The Squid's Tentacle Grapple stacks with Evasive Maneuver. Combined, you can survive terrifying enemy turns with minimal damage.
• Man-o-War Broadside on a full reload cycle deals more burst damage than any other single action in the game — save it for boss fights.
• Drain enemy crew with the Psionic Lance on tough enemies — at 0 crew they lose their specials and deal reduced damage.
• The Hammership Ram has self-damage. Don't use it when you're already low on hull.
